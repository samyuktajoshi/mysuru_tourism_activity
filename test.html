Namma Mysuru

Mysuru, also known as Mysore, is a historic city in the southern state of Karnataka, India. Renowned for its rich cultural heritage, palaces, and festivities, Mysuru was once the capital of the Kingdom of Mysore, ruled by the Wadiyar dynasty. It is famous for its grand Mysore Palace, a magnificent architectural marvel that attracts tourists from around the world.

In addition to its palaces, Mysuru is known for its vibrant Dasara festival, celebrated with grandeur and pomp, featuring processions, cultural performances, and illuminated streets. The city is also a center for traditional crafts such as silk weaving and sandalwood carving.

Mysuru boasts several other attractions including the Chamundi Hill with its ancient Chamundeshwari Temple, the tranquil Brindavan Gardens, and the historic St. Philomena's Cathedral.

Today, Mysuru is a bustling city with a blend of tradition and modernity, offering visitors a glimpse into its rich cultural past while embracing the conveniences of contemporary life.

How to reach ?

Mysore is one of the more well-connected cities in Karnataka and is accessible through road, rail and air. Although Mysore has its own airport, it is not fully functional and doesn't connect to all major cities. So tourists can access Mysore through the Bangalore airport (170km). Getting on a train to the Mysore railway station is a very convenient way of travel as daily trains ply here. Bus services are also regularly running and finding a seat is never a problem.

Mysore Junction (MYS), also known as Mysuru Junction, is the primary railway station in the city. Other stations include Ashokapuram (AP).
Roadways are preferred for reaching Mysore. The city's main bus gateway is Mysore City Bus Stand, with KSRTC Bus Stand serving as the interstate terminal.
Mysore Airport (MYQ) serves direct flights to cities including Bangalore, Hyderabad, Chennai, Goa, and Kochis. Kempegowda International Airport (BLR) serves as the nearest entry point from other cities.
