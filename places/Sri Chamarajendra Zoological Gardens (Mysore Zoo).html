Sri Chamarajendra Zoological Gardens (Mysore Zoo)

Sri Chamarajendra Zoological Gardens, more commonly known as Mysore Zoo, is one of the oldest and most well-maintained zoos in India, situated in the city of Mysore, Karnataka. Established in 1892 under the royal patronage of the Wodeyar dynasty, it spans across an expansive area of around 157 acres, providing a habitat for a diverse range of wildlife species. The zoo is renowned for its conservation efforts and breeding programs aimed at preserving endangered species. Home to over 150 species of animals, including exotic and indigenous ones, Mysore Zoo offers visitors an immersive experience into the world of wildlife. Some of its star attractions include Bengal tigers, Indian elephants, white rhinoceroses, and African lions. Beyond its role in conservation and education, the zoo also serves as a recreational destination, drawing tourists and locals alike with its lush greenery, well-designed enclosures, and educational exhibits. Mysore Zoo stands as a testament to the city's rich heritage and commitment to wildlife conservation, making it a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts and animal lovers.
