Brindavan Gardens

Brindavan Gardens, located in the city of Mysore in the southern state of Karnataka, India, is a renowned and picturesque garden spread over an area of about 60 acres. Constructed in the 1930s, these gardens are situated adjacent to the Krishnarajasagara Dam across the river Kaveri. The garden's layout is inspired by the Mughal style and features terraces, fountains, lush lawns, and a myriad of colorful flowers that bloom throughout the year, creating a mesmerizing sight for visitors. One of the main attractions of Brindavan Gardens is the musical fountain, which comes alive in the evenings with synchronized music and colorful lights, enchanting spectators with its rhythmic water dance. The gardens also offer boating facilities on the serene waters of the adjoining lake, allowing visitors to enjoy a peaceful ride amidst the scenic beauty. Brindavan Gardens is not only a popular tourist destination but also serves as a recreational spot for locals, providing a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.
